Why this matters

The Caspian Sea is part of Azerbaijan’s identity, nature, tourism, and everyday life. But waste from streets, parks, beaches, drainage areas, and coastal spaces can move toward the sea. A plastic bottle or food package left on land may seem small, but when many people do the same thing, it becomes a much larger pollution problem.

Many people care about the Caspian Sea, but they do not always know where pollution is happening or what they can do about it. Caspian Alert turns the problem into something visible: people can report polluted places, see them on a real map, and help decide where awareness or cleanup action is needed first.

Small reports can create a bigger picture.

People report polluted places with a location, waste type, and optional photo.

Map

Reports appear as colored hotspot markers: low, medium, or urgent priority.

Act

The website suggests practical next steps, such as awareness, monitoring, or cleanup.

Spreading Awareness

The first goal of this prototype is not only to collect reports. It is also to help young people understand that pollution does not appear in the sea by accident. Many polluted spots begin with normal daily habits: using disposable plastic, leaving snack packaging outside, ignoring litter near drains, or assuming that someone else will clean it later.

From street to sea

Waste left on streets or near drains can be moved by wind, rain, or people until it reaches coastal areas and the Caspian Sea.

Wildlife impact

Plastic and other waste can harm birds, fish, and Caspian wildlife. Even small items can become dangerous when they enter natural habitats.

Community pride

Clean beaches, parks, and seaside spaces make the community healthier, more beautiful, and more welcoming for families and visitors.

Individual action

Individuals may not be able to solve the whole problem alone, but they can help make pollution visible and encourage others to act.

Awareness message for the campaign

“If we can see the problem, we can start solving it. By reporting polluted places, the community helps protect the Caspian Sea one clean spot at a time.”

How priority is calculated

The score is based on:

  • amount of waste
  • whether it is close to sea, drain, or water flow
  • whether the place is repeatedly polluted
  • waste type, especially plastic or mixed waste

The goal is not perfect science. The goal is to help the society decide which places need attention first.
